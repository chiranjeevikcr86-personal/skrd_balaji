SRI KANUKONDARAYA SWAMY DEVASTHANAM

(A Manifestation of Sri Lord Venkateswara swamy)

Location: Pedakanti Palli (Village), Gangadhara Nellore Mandal, Chittoor District, Andhra Pradesh- 517125.

The Eternal Identity: Lord Vishnu as the Sacred Ox

A Divine Distinction: Beyond the Vahana

A defining attribute of the Sri Kanukondaraya Swamy Devasthanam is its unique and profound theological significance. In the broader landscape of Hindu tradition, the Ox is almost universally revered as Maha Nandi, the sacred vehicle (Vahana) and loyal messenger of Lord Shiva. However, our temple preserves an ancient and distinct truth: here, the Devara Eddu is not a vehicle, but a direct, living manifestation (Vibhavarupa) of Lord Maha Vishnu (Lord Venkateswara swamy).

The Unique Divine Identity: Lord Vishnu in the Form of the Sacred Ox

A defining feature of this temple is its unique theological significance. In most Hindu temples, the Ox is worshipped as Maha Nandi, the sacred vehicle (Vahana) of Lord Shiva. However, in our tradition, the Devara Eddu is not a vehicle; it is worshipped as a direct manifestation of Lord Maha Vishnu (Lord Venkateswara Swamy).

The Walking God

While other shrines may house statues or symbols, the devotees of Pedakanti Palli believe that the Lord Himself walks among them in the form of the Sacred Ox. This is not merely an animal representing the Divine; it is the Supreme Presence of Lord Kanukondaraya manifesting in a form of humility, strength, and grace. To witness the Devara Eddu is to receive the Darshan of Lord Venkateswara in a physical, breathing form—a "Walking God" who surveys His lands and protects His people just as He did during the Great Drought of the north.

The Sacred Confluence: Bridging Hari and Hara

Our temple serves as a rare "Sacred Confluence" of faith, where the two great streams of Hinduism—Vaishnavism and Shaivism—meet in perfect harmony.

The Harmony of Maha Shivaratri: We celebrate Maha Shivaratri with immense grandness and fervor. While this night is traditionally dedicated to Lord Shiva, we observe it to honor the divine bridge between traditions.

The Unity of Form and Soul: It is a spiritual marvel where a form typically associated with Shiva's service (the Ox) is filled with the soul and sovereign grace of Vishnu. In this sacred space, the boundaries of sect dissolve, leaving only pure devotion.

The Divine Manifestation & The Three Steps

The origin of the Sri Kanukondaraya Swamy Devasthanam is a miraculous saga of survival that has been preserved in our hearts and through our lineage for over 1,000 years.

The Great Exodus (19th Century Context)

While the spiritual roots of our faith reach back over a millennium, the pivotal manifestation of the Devara Eddu is deeply tied to the historical resilience of our people. Around the 19th Century, the region of Mulakalacheruvu (Annamayya District) was gripped by a catastrophic, life-threatening drought. As the earth cracked and the people despaired, Lord Venkateswara manifested His grace in the form of a Sacred Ox (Devara Eddu) to lead the community toward life and water

The Three Eternal Steps

Step 1 – Kalavagunta (Penumuru Mandal, Chittoor)

The Landing of Divine Grace: The first miraculous step was taken in Kalavagunta. It was here that the Devara Eddu first signaled its presence, marking the beginning of the exodus from the drought-stricken north. This site represents the first spark of hope and the moment the Lord’s grace touched the earth to lead His people.

Step 2 – Kaligiri Konda (Penumuru Mandal , Chittoor)

The Miraculous Leap: As the journey continued, the Devara Eddu reached the base of the majestic Kaligiri hills. In a display of divine power, the Ox took a miraculous leap from the plains directly to the hilltop. This second imprint, etched into the high peaks, serves as a testament to the supernatural strength and celestial nature of the guide. To this day, the hilltop is revered as a site of immense spiritual energy.

Step 3 – Pedakanti Palli

The Final Destination: The third and final step was taken in Pedakanti Palli. At the exact moment this footprint was made, the drought broke, and the land was blessed with water and abundance. Recognizing this as the "Chosen Land," the Lord settled here, and the people established their home under His protection.

Identification & Miracles of the Devara Eddu

The Devara Eddu is not a common animal but a manifestation of Lord Maha Vishnu. Its divinity is verified through specific miraculous behaviors

The Saturday Cycle: Miraculously, the Sacred Ox is destined to be born only on a Saturday and will eventually pass away only on a Saturday, bookending its mission on the Lord’s most auspicious day.

Spiritual Purity at Birth: The moment of birth, the sacred calf provides a spiritual signal by instinctively refusing to take milk, distinguishing itself from all other oxen. If the household is deemed spiritually "impure" for instance, if a woman in the home is in her menstrual period the calf will not only refuse the milk but will immediately depart from the owner’s home, remaining away until spiritual balance is restored. Conversely, even when the household is perfectly pure, the calf continues to refuse its mother's milk initially; this persistent behavior serves as divine proof that the calf belongs neither to the owner nor the mother cow, but is dedicated solely to the service of the Lord

The Divine Dream: The Lord reveals the calf's specific location to the elders of the community specifically to the Chinna Kamasani lineage through prophetic dreams. This lineage carries the spiritual responsibility of recognizing the Lord's chosen vessel.

The Acceptance Ritual: The elders travel to the calf’s home to perform the Acceptance Ritual, involving a formal Pooja, the breaking of coconuts, and offerings of flowers. Only upon the completion of this ceremony does the calf return to the Mother Cow and accept its milk, signaling its divine acceptance of its role as the Lord's representative

The 3rd Year Velpu Utsavam: Once identified and accepted, a grand Velpu Utsavam is performed in the ox’s third year to officially consecrate its life of service to the temple and the community.

The Three Historical Phases of the Devara Eddu

The lineage of the Sacred Ox is not merely a story; it is a documented history of divine service. While modern documentation exists for the 20th century, village records and elder testimonies confirm the tradition was active well before the 1900s. Since 1933, our community has recorded three distinct phases of the Sacred Ox, each serving as the living representative of Lord Sri Kanukondaraya Swamy.

Phase 1: The Era of Foundation (1933 – 1955)

The Beginning: This phase officially established the modern Velpu Utsavam tradition that we celebrate today.

Historical Record: Our temple archives contain the sacred invitation dated June 18, 1933, which stands as the earliest formal documentation of our grand festivals.

Conclusion: This first Sacred Ox served the community faithfully for 22 years before passing away in 1955, leaving behind a legacy of faith that would sustain the village for decades to come.

Phase 2: The Golden Era (1987 – 2004)

Birth & Service: Born on June 1, 1987, this Sacred Ox was recognized by the elders and served the Lord for a glorious 18 years.

A Period of Prosperity: This era saw a significant rise in the temple's spiritual influence across the region.

The Great Honor: Following its passing in late 2004, the community organized a massive Maha Shanti Yajnam on November 27, 2005. Thousands of devotees gathered to pay their final respects during the sacred burial (Bhoo-sthapitam).

Phase 3: The Brief Blessing (2014 – 2015)

The Recognition: On October 25, 2014, the newest Sacred Ox was identified. Following our ancient protocols, it was officially "taken up" into the Lord's service on November 27, 2014.

The Tragedy: Sadly, this phase was short-lived. Within the same year, the Ox tragically passed away due to lung failure highlighting the fragile and precious nature of the divine connection.

Legacy: Though its time was brief, this Ox reaffirmed the community's commitment to the tradition,

Fulfilling the divine cycle, the sacred animal is destined to pass away only on a Saturday, bookending its earthly mission on the Lord’s most auspicious day.

The Mall: Sacred Memorial & Pallaru Chekka

Located just 500 meters from the main temple in Maredupalli Village, The Mall serves as the spiritual anchor for the chinna kamasani community. It is the hallowed ground where the physical remains of the Lord's divine messengers rest, and where our ancient ancestral rituals continue to thrive.

The Sacred Concept of Pallaru Chekka

The Mall is the center for the worship of Pallaru Chekka, a profound spiritual concept passed down through generations. It represents a divine state of universal harmony, depicting the unity between:

The Cow & The Ox: Symbols of life, sustenance, and the Lord’s chosen vessels.

The Tiger: Representing the wild, powerful forces of nature.

The Devotee: Representing the human soul in total surrender to the Divine.

In this sacred space, it is believed that under the grace of Lord Sri Kanukondaraya Swamy, the predator (Tiger) and the prey (Ox) exist together in absolute peace. This symbol serves as a reminder that divine protection can harmonize even the most opposing forces of nature.

The Ancestral Graveyard (Samadhi)

The Mall is the official Ancestral Graveyard where the Sacred Oxen of the past phases are laid to rest. Each Samadhi is treated with the highest reverence, as these animals are considered part of the Lord’s divine family.

Ancestral Legacy & Modern Transformation

The survival and growth of the temple are a testament to the selfless devotion of our ancestors and the continued support of the state.

The foundation of the temple's wealth and sustainability was laid by our forefathers, specifically the Chinna Kamasani lineage. They donated over 60 acres of land to the temple. This immense gift was intended to ensure that the rituals, the care of the Sacred Oxen, and the service to Lord Sri Kanukondaraya Swamy would continue for eternity without financial burden.

Government Guardianship

Recognizing the temple's growing regional importance and the need for professional preservation, the administration was formally handed over to the Andhra Pradesh Endowment Department in 10.03.1977

Today, the temple is under state protection and is managed by a government-appointed Executive Officer (EO).

This partnership ensures that the temple's lands and traditions are legally protected and managed with transparency.

Reconstruction from Scratch

We are currently witnessing the most significant physical transformation in our history. To accommodate the growing faith of over 1,200 devotees (referred to as “Thalakattu,” derived from the Tamil word “thalai kattu,” meaning headcount) and the thousands of visiting pilgrims

  • The Project: The original main temple in Pedakuntapalli is being entirely rebuilt from scratch.

The Site: The new structure is being constructed on an expanded 2-acre site to create a grander environment for worship.

Investment: This is a Rs.7 Crore government-sanctioned project (initiated in 2020), representing a historic investment in our spiritual heritage.

The completion of this grand temple in 2028 will mark the beginning of a new era, ensuring that the miraculous story of the Three Steps and the Devara Eddu lives on for centuries to come.
