The Eternal Identity

Lord Vishnu as the Sacred Ox

A Divine Distinction

In the broader landscape of Hindu tradition, the Ox is almost universally revered as Maha Nandi — the sacred vehicle of Lord Shiva. Our temple preserves an ancient and distinct truth: here, the Devara Eddu is not a vehicle, but a direct, living manifestation (Vibhavarupa) of Lord Maha Vishnu.

The Walking God

While other shrines house statues or symbols, the devotees of Pedakanti Palli believe that the Lord Himself walks among them in the form of the Sacred Ox. To witness the Devara Eddu is to receive the Darshan of Lord Venkateswara in a physical, breathing form.

The Sacred Confluence

Our temple serves as a rare "Sacred Confluence" of faith — where the two great streams of Hinduism, Vaishnavism and Shaivism, meet in perfect harmony. It is a spiritual marvel where a form typically associated with Shiva's service is filled with the sovereign grace of Vishnu.

19th Century · The Great Exodus

The Divine Three Steps

Around the 19th Century, the region of Mulakalacheruvu (Annamayya District) was gripped by a catastrophic, life-threatening drought. As the earth cracked and the people despaired, Lord Venkateswara manifested His grace in the form of a Sacred Ox — the Devara Eddu — to lead the community toward life and water.

01

Step 01 · Penumuru Mandal, Chittoor

Kalavagunta

The Landing of Divine Grace

The first miraculous step was taken in Kalavagunta. It was here that the Devara Eddu first signaled its presence, marking the beginning of the exodus from the drought-stricken north. This site represents the first spark of hope and the moment the Lord's grace touched the earth.

02

Step 02 · Penumuru Mandal, Chittoor

Kaligiri Konda

The Miraculous Leap

As the journey continued, the Devara Eddu reached the base of the majestic Kaligiri hills. In a display of divine power, the Ox took a miraculous leap from the plains directly to the hilltop. This second imprint, etched into the high peaks, serves as a testament to the supernatural strength of the sacred guide.

03

Step 03 · Gangadhara Nellore Mandal, Chittoor

Pedakanti Palli

The Final Destination

The third and final step was taken in Pedakanti Palli. At the exact moment this footprint was made, the drought broke, and the land was blessed with water and abundance. Recognizing this as the "Chosen Land", the Lord settled here, and the people established their home under His protection.

Documented Sacred History

Three Historical Phases

🏛️

Phase 1

1933 – 1955

The Era of Foundation

  • Officially established the modern Velpu Utsavam tradition
  • Earliest formal archive: sacred festival invitation dated June 18, 1933
  • This Devara Eddu served faithfully for 22 years before passing in 1955
✨

Phase 2

1987 – 2004

The Golden Era

  • Born on June 1, 1987 — identified by the Chinna Kamasani elders
  • Served the Lord for 18 glorious years — a period of regional spiritual rise
  • Maha Shanti Yajnam held Nov 27, 2005 — thousands paid final respects
🕊️

Phase 3

2014 – 2015

The Brief Blessing

  • Identified on Oct 25, 2014 — taken up into service Nov 27, 2014
  • Tragically short-lived — passed due to lung failure within the year
  • Yet it reaffirmed the community's sacred commitment to the tradition

Chinna Kamasani Lineage

Ancestral Legacy

🌾

60 Acres Donated

The foundation of the temple's sustainability was laid by the Chinna Kamasani lineage, who donated over 60 acres of land to ensure rituals and service to the Sacred Oxen would continue for eternity without financial burden.

🏛️

Government Guardianship

On March 10, 1977, the Andhra Pradesh Endowment Department formally assumed administration — ensuring legally protected management with transparency and professional preservation.

🕉️

Maha Shivaratri Celebrations

The temple celebrates Maha Shivaratri on a grand scale — honoring the divine bridge between Vaishnavism and Shaivism. Note: Grand celebrations are temporarily suspended during the 2020–2028 reconstruction.

Pallaru Chekka
